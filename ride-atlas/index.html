METALLURGY MATTERS

THE SCIENCE OF THE SCREAM

THE HOLY QUARTET OF METALS

  • Titanium (Grade 1-4): 40% lighter than steel. Turns blue/purple under heat. The MotoGP standard.
  • Inconel: A superalloy that survives in jet engines. Retains strength at 1000°C+.
  • Stainless Steel (304/316): The workhorse. Durable, corrosion-resistant, but heavy.
  • Carbon Fiber: Used for mufflers and heat shields. Maximum weight saving and cool to the touch.

WHY METALLURGY CHANGES EVERYTHING

It's not just about weight. Thinner titanium walls (0.6mm - 1mm) vibrate at higher frequencies, giving that iconic "F1-like" scream. Stainless steel, being denser, produces a deeper, bassier thrum.

Heat Dissipation: Titanium sheds heat 3x faster than steel, meaning your exhaust is cold to the touch minutes after a hard ride.

HOW TO MINT YOUR OWN EXHAUST

Custom 'minting' a system requires precision TIG Welding with Argon Purging to prevent oxidation inside the pipe.

  1. Design: Use 3D scanning to map your bike's header ports.
  2. Bending: Mandrel bending ensures the inner diameter stays consistent for laminar gas flow.
  3. Welding: "Stack of dimes" welds are the hallmark of a master minter.
  4. Tuning: Pair with a Power Commander or ECU flash to 'mint' the perfect air-fuel ratio for the new flow.
